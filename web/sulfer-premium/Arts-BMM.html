Bachelor of Mass Media(BMM)

Bachelor of Mass Media

What is Bachelor of Mass Media?

  • BMM or Bachelor of Mass media is one of the most sought-after courses for Class 12th students.
  • BMM is an undergraduate level three-year duration course.
  • It includes the study of different channels of communication such as newspapers, television, radio, and internet.
  • In today’s scenario, media stands as an attractive career option to the Indian youth and a degree in BMM offers a wide range of job opportunities to candidates. BMM teaches students all the technical skills required to be successful in the field of media.
  • The course also imparts other essential skills such as research aptitude and verbal as well as presentation skills to candidates.

Bachelor of Mass Media

All candidates who have cleared Class 12th in any stream from a recognised board with at least 50% - 60% marks fulfill eligibility criteria for BMM course. It may be noted that the eligibility criteria mentioned above might vary depending on the policy of the college/ university in which a candidate is applying.

There are a wide variety of jobs available for BMM graduates in the fields of television, films, public relations, publishing, journalism, and so on. Salary that a BMM graduate gets, however, depends on different factors such as institute from which a candidate has obtained the degree, profile of the employer, job experience, etc. Given below are some of the popular job profiles that BMM graduates can think of taking up: Director: A director is responsible for controlling the dramatic as well as artistic aspects of a film or TV. The director visualizes the screenplay and guides the actors and supporting staff in fulfilment of that vision. Sound Engineer: A sound engineer operates as well as maintains all the technical equipment used to record, amplify or mix sound. A sound engineer has a key role in identifying the sound requirements for a given project and perform necessary actions to produce the sound. Event Manager: An event manager is responsible for managing a whole event. An event manager coordinates details of an event and manages the whole process starting from planning day to running the actual day and the post-event evaluation. Journalist: The basic responsibility of a journalist is to research, document, write as well as present news in an honest and ethical manner. A journalist spends much of his/ her timeinterviewing sources, searching other sources for information, and sometimes visiting the scene where a newsworthy event has happened. Columnist: A columnist is a professional who writes and edits copies for a regular column in a newspaper. A columnist shares information of public interest and provides opinions to their readers. Content Writer: A content writer researches industry-related topics using online sources, interviews, etc and writes content usually to promote a product or service. A content writer uses different web formatting tools and content management systems to create work. News Archor A news anchor has the responsibility of presenting news stories on radio or television broadcasts. Apart from this, an anchor also selects and analyzes stories, and interviews guests. Photographer: Using the artistic ability along with technical skills, a photographer takes pictures of people, places, landscapes, food, and a lot more. A photographer usually works with the clients to discuss the type of pictures they need and how they wish to use them. Proofreader: A proofreader is a professional who has the responsibility of ensuring that any content that is being published in a newspaper or magazine is error-free. A proofreader reads a copy and makes sure there are no spelling, grammatical or typographical mistakes
