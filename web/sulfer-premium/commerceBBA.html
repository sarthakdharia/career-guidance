BBA Course:

BBA is an undergraduate Business Management Course. The full form of BBA is "Bachelor of Business Administration." BBA Course is designed to provide a basic understanding of management education and to train the students in communication skills effectively, which inculcate entrepreneurship skills and decision making. The course duration for BBA is 3 years with six semesters. The students are having an ample opportunity for jobs being newly created in the management profession. BBA Course Preparation Tips: Here are a few common preparation tips candidates should follow to clear the entrance exams for Bachelor of Business Administration [BBA] are • Understanding the paper pattern: The foremost step in preparing well for the exam is a comprehensive analysis and explanation of the question paper format. • Adhering to a timetable: Focus points should be kept in mind, with particular emphasis on weaker subjects and the daily practice of numerical problems. A timetable should be drawn and the candidate should follow the timetable with dedication and diligence. • Enrolling in coaching institutes: A coaching class or a tutor especially for clearing the entrance exam is a bonus as the teacher will help a candidate to focus on weaker zones and will aid in practice and comprehension of topics. Practice makes perfect: Candidates should practice and revise before the exam so that it reduces the unnecessary pressure one day before the examination.

Bachelor of Business Administration [BBA] is an undergraduate course which is present in most of the colleges in India. The minimum eligibility for getting BBA admission is 10+2 with 45% to 60% it varies according to the college. The approximate age required to pursue BBA is minimum 17 to a maximum of 25 years. English is a compulsory subject for pursuing BBA in any college otherwise both arts and science stream students can apply for the course The students who have written the higher secondary exams and waiting for the results may also apply for this particular course. The cutoff percentage differs from institute to institute. There are entrance exams like IPU CET, NMAT, etc. to get admitted into prestigious institutes.
